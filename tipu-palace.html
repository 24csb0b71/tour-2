Tipu Sultan Palace

Tipu Sultan’s Summer Palace is a historical landmark in Bangalore, known for its Indo-Islamic architecture. Built in the late 18th century, this palace served as the summer residence of Tipu Sultan, the ruler of Mysore.

The palace features intricate teakwood carvings, arches, balconies, and frescoes depicting the grandeur of Tipu Sultan’s era. It is now a museum showcasing artifacts related to his reign.
